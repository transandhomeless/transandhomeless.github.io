Colorado

If you make less than $1,507/month consider applying to Health First Colorado. It will cover all trans related healthcare, including therapy appointments, HRT, and surgeries.

If you make less than $2,148/month consider applying for SNAP benefits (money for food).

Colorado Name Change Project

Helps change legal name and gender marker

Arapahoe County

Comitis Crisis Center

Provides emergency shelter to adults. Offers meals, hygiene items, and seasonal clothing, showers, referral services and case management, healthcare screenings, and LGBTQA+ affirming supportive services.

The Don't Look Back Center

Has a transitional sober living home for trans women.

Boulder County

Out Boulder County

Has support groups, free bus cards, and a gender affirming closet (including binders) for youth ages 11 to 18. Has mental health services, free bus passes, and support groups for people of all ages.

Safe Shelter of St. Vrain Valley

Provides emergency shelter, individual and group counseling, case management and legal advocacy to victims of domestic violence.

Denver County

The Center on Colfax

Has HIV/STI screenings, insurance help, mental health counseling, social support groups, fitness classes, technology, and a library.

The Gathering Place

Offers meals, showers, laundry, phone access, mail access, physical and mental health services, a nap room (where members can rest during the day) and a clothing bank. Helps members get a High School Equivalency degree, find a job, and has a case management program to help homeless members.

The Delores Project

Provides emergency shelter and permanent supportive housing to adults.

Urban Peak

Provides shelter and housing for youth ages 15 to 20. Offers three meals a day, a case manager (to help find housing and other resources), laundry services, showers, access to computers, help with obtaning a GED or re-enrolling in high school, applying to college; and help with obtaining a job.

El Paso County

Inside Out

Has support groups, mental health services and resources to youth ages 13 to 24.

The Place

Provides shelter, meals, clothing, and health services to youth ages 15 to 20. Has a drop in center for youth ages 15 to 24.

Springs Rescue Mission

Provides shelter and meals to adults. Allows pets.

Just Living Recovery

Sober living home
